01 Approach

Landscapes that feel grown, not imposed

I believe landscape design emerges from the dialogue between place, architecture and human experience. My approach combines naturalistic planting, ecological sensitivity and atmospheric composition.

I am particularly interested in perennial communities, contextual planting and outdoor spaces that feel emotionally connected to their environment — working with the existing site rather than against it, and letting a landscape develop over time.

02 Selected Work

Forest Landscape Restoration

Production & Implementation · Restoration Strategy · 2.5 ha · 3 years involvement

The work centred on restoring the understorey after construction — preserving mature birches and rebuilding layered plant communities so the forest could settle back over the landscape.

From concept and planting design through plant sourcing to author supervision on site: geoplastics, paths threaded delicately through the trees, meadow and waterside zones, and a long-term planting strategy followed across three years of change.

  • Constructed 2020–2022
  • 50+ tree & shrub species · 95 perennial species
  • 32,157 perennials & ornamental grasses planted
Before / After

A warning before we begin: this story is not a short one. Please, settle into a comfortable seat, bring a cup of something good, and enjoy the ride. Whether this work becomes your cup of tea or not, I hope you'll find something interesting along the way.

Zones of Interest

The 2.5-hectare site is shaped by an existing birch woodland that occupies more than one third of the property. The landscape concept began with this woodland, using ecological restoration and biodiversity enhancement as a framework for the site's development.

A series of distinct landscapes emerge within the woodland setting: shaded fern gardens, berry meadows, rolling grassy landforms, and riverside walks. A boardwalk connects these experiences, leading visitors through changing atmospheres towards the river and its seasonal evening mists.

The Estate Garden

Conceptual Design & Art Direction · private residence · pine-forest context

A contemporary landscape concept for a premium private residence set within an existing pine forest. The project explores the relationship between strong architectural geometry and soft, naturalistic planting.

The house is integrated into its forest context through spatial layering, material continuity and ecological planting strategies — developed as a full conception, visualization and presentation package with hand-rendered masterplans and axonometrics.

  • Conceptual design
  • Vertical planning
  • Atmospheric illustration
  • Project submission

Foundation: Architecture

The landscape is conceived as an extension of the house architecture. New outdoor rooms emerge from the building geometry, creating a cohesive spatial experience.

Château Character

Inspired by the elegance and composure of a French château. The reference informs atmosphere and spatial structure rather than stylistic imitation.

Reflections

Architectural lines, proportions, and details are echoed throughout the landscape. A continuous dialogue between built form and garden.

Classic Contemporary

A contemporary interpretation of classical garden principles. Strong geometry softened by planting and seasonal dynamics.

03 About

A planting-focused practice, rooted in concept and storytelling

I am a landscape designer with a background in graphic design, combining planting design, visual storytelling, and spatial thinking.

Inspired by contemporary European landscape practice, I create gardens and landscapes that are rooted in their place, shaped by plants, and designed to change beautifully over time.

Alongside design work, I continue to develop hands-on horticultural knowledge through volunteering at the Botanical Garden in Belgrade and ongoing study of perennial and climate-adapted planting communities.
